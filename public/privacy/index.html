Privacy Policy

Rough Cut Manufacturing · Last updated June 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Rough Cut Manufacturing ("we," "us") handles information in our internal operations and accounting application (the "App"), including its integration with QuickBooks Online via Intuit.

Who uses this app

The App is an internal business tool used by authorized employees and administrators of Rough Cut Manufacturing. It is not a consumer product and is not offered to the general public.

Information we process

We process business records needed to run our operations and books, including:

  • Account/user profile data for authentication and access control.
  • Job, inventory, quoting, and scheduling data.
  • Accounting data synchronized from our QuickBooks Online company through the Intuit API — chart of accounts, customers, vendors, items, invoices, bills, payments, estimates, and journal entries.

QuickBooks Online (Intuit) integration

When an administrator connects the App to QuickBooks, Intuit provides secure OAuth tokens that let the App read data from our own QuickBooks company. Those tokens are stored server-side and are never exposed to the browser. We use the connection only to mirror our own accounting data into the App; we do not access any company other than our own, and the App does not write to QuickBooks.

How we use information

Information is used solely to provide internal operations, job costing, and accounting functionality. We do not sell personal information, and we do not share it with third parties except service providers that host or process data on our behalf (for example, our database and hosting providers) under appropriate safeguards.

Data retention and security

Data is retained as long as needed for business and recordkeeping purposes. We use industry-standard measures including encrypted transport (HTTPS), row-level access controls, and restricted credentials to protect data.

Disconnecting

An administrator may disconnect QuickBooks at any time from the App's integrations page, which removes the stored authorization. Disconnecting does not delete data already imported.
